Information about SDG 1

SDG 1 aims to end poverty in all its forms everywhere. Despite progress, about 9.2% of the world's workers who live with family members made less than $1.90 per person per day in 2017. Achieving SDG 1 requires addressing various challenges such as:

  • Lack of access to education and healthcare
  • Income inequality
  • Unemployment and underemployment
  • Discrimination and social exclusion

These challenges need to be tackled through effective policies and interventions at local, national, and global levels.

Solutions and how you can help

There are several ways individuals can contribute to achieving SDG 1 and reducing poverty:

  • Supporting organizations that provide access to education, healthcare, and social services
  • Promoting fair trade and ethical consumption
  • Advocating for policies that ensure fair wages, social protection, and economic opportunities for all
  • Volunteering with local community organizations to address poverty-related issues
  • Raising awareness about the root causes and consequences of poverty

By taking these actions, individuals can make a positive impact and contribute to building a more inclusive and equitable society.
